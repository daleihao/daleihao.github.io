About Me

I am an Earth Scientist in the Atmospheric, Climate, & Earth Sciences Division at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL), where I work at the intersection of land surface modeling, remote sensing, and Earth system science.

My central focus is advancing the E3SM Land Model (ELM) by incorporating physically realistic representations of sub-grid topography, snow processes (grain shape, light-absorbing particles), and radiation–topography feedbacks. These improvements reduce uncertainty in projections of snowpack, water resources, and surface energy balance across complex terrain such as the Tibetan Plateau and Sierra Nevada.

I also develop novel remote sensing methods for estimating solar radiation and solar-induced chlorophyll fluorescence (SIF) from satellite observations (DSCOVR/EPIC), with applications to global vegetation monitoring, the terrestrial carbon cycle, and photosynthesis.

My work has been highlighted in Nature and covered by FOX Weather, ScienceDaily, GIZMODO, Yahoo News, Mongabay, and more. I serve as Editor of Earth System Science Data (ESSD) and Topic Editor for Remote Sensing.
